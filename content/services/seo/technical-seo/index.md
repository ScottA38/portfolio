### What is SEO?

[**SEO**](https://mailchimp.com/marketing-glossary/seo/) stands for **'Search Engine Optimisation'**. 

SEO is the process of making calculated changes to a website's design (i.e: layout design, content design, site performance etc.) in order to increase your website's chance of **becoming a prime recommendation** for _specific keywords_ or _key-phrases_ relevant to your target audience.

If I owned a business which manufactures lightweight titanium Mountain bike parts in the U.K, for example, I may want to appear on the first page of Search Engine Results Pages ([SERPs](https://mailchimp.com/marketing-glossary/serp/)) for phrases like "Titanium bike wheels UK", "Lightweight bike parts" or "lightweight bike forks".

Generally speaking, though, the more generic a search term is, the more fierce the competition will be for search rankings within it and the harder it will be to reach people using that criteria.

#### What factors lead to success in SEO?

Modern Search Engines have advanced rapidly in the last decade in order to accomodate to the increased demands of their users to find relevant information quickly, as well as to adapt to the [sheer volume of websites](https://www.networksolutions.com/blog/how-many-domain-names-are-there/) available on the internet today.

Today, matching for keywords in a specific search query is simply the tip of the metaphorical iceberg, with Search engines now considering a huge range of holistic factors in their calculations, from _text colour contrasts_ to _web page performance benchmarks_. Effective SEO management for such [advanced search algorithms](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/googlebot) involves the consideration of a wide range of holistic factors which affect search positioning.
